Cultural and Heritage Places

Sigiriya

Sigiriya or Sinhagiri is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. It is a site of historical and archaeological significance that is dominated by a massive column of rock approximately 180 metres high. Sigiriya, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a prime example of ancient urban planning. Also known as the Lion Rock, this place is known around the world for the ruins of the ancient Sigiriya rock fort and its fascinating and well-preserved attractions.

The most prominent feature of the rock is the lion staircase leading to the palace garden. The lion can be visualized as a giant figure standing against a granite rock. The lion's open mouth leads to a staircase made of brick and wood. However, only the two feet and the masonry walls around it remain in this majestic structure. Even so, the cuts on the rock and the plantation give the impression of a lion figure

Temple of the Tooth Relic

Known as the Temple of the Tooth, it is considered to be the most sacred Buddhist temple in the world. The Tooth Relic of the Buddha is housed in a golden chamber and is located on the premises of the Royal Palace (formerly a princely state). Kandy is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the Tooth Relic is said to hold an essential place in the governance of any country.

The interior environment is exceptionally comfortable and welcoming; It smells of fresh flowers and is lit with coconut oil candles and lamps. Daily offerings are made in the basement of the temple. Relics bathe with a medicinal plant on Wednesdays.

The temple grounds are spread over several hectares and all the tourist spots are taken under one head. The Museum, the Royal Gardens, the Royal Palace, the Lounge, the Palace, the Meditation Center, the Moonstone and many other types of Kandy Lake flowers are on display - these and many more are a testament to its splendor and grandeur!

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is a historical paradise as it has many heritage monasteries, palaces and monuments. It is famous for its large dagobas or brick stupas, ancient ponds and pools and magnificent temples. It was also during this period that Anuradhapura came to the fore as a seat of political and economic importance.

Apart from Ruwanweliseya, the most popular tourist attractions in Anuradhapura are its ruins or the Anuradhapura World Heritage Site, the sacred Bo tree, the Lowamahapaya, the Archaeological Museum, the Isurumuniya Vihara, the Ratna Prasadaya or the Gem Palace or the Royal Amusement Park. , Buddha's Samadhi, Royal Palace and much more. The most famous dagobas are Ruwanweli, Mirisaweti, Abhayagiri, Jethavanarama, Thuparama, Lankarama and many more. It is interesting to note that the monasteries in Anuradhapura cover a vast area of ​​16 square miles. Thus, Anuradhapura seems to be culturally and religiously unimaginably important.

True Buddhist Traditions, Archaeological Ruins, Luxury Palaces, Traditional Monasteries and Museums of Information define the greatness of Anuradhapura. Despite being a big city, Anuradhapura gives the impression of being in a village. Anuradhapura is a well-established tourist attraction in Sri Lanka with well-preserved cultural sites, ponds and monuments. Tourists come to this city for a refreshing break, to seek peace and to satisfy their curiosity.

Polonnaruwa

During the reign of King Parakramabahu the Great, such heroic scales of paddy cultivation were sustained by the construction of a vast irrigation network of naturally occurring reservoirs in the Great Sea. Sri Lanka became known as the granary of the East. Polonnaruwa consists of the ruins of the glorious kingdom of King Parakramabahu the Great. The richness and beauty of this kingdom are still evident. Today, the ancient city of Polonnaruwa remains one of the best planned archeological sites in the country, bearing witness to the discipline and greatness of the first rulers of the kingdom.

When you going to visit polonnaaruwa you can visit Gal Vihara, Parakrama Samudraya (Sea of Parakrama), Rankoth Vihara, King Parakramabahu Palace, Thivanka Image House, Ancient Library (Pothgul Vihara) and etc.

Dambulla Cave Temple

Dambulla is the largest and best preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka. The rock towers are 160 meters higher than the surrounding plains. There are more than 80 documented caves in the surrounding area. The main attractions are spread over five caves containing statues and paintings. These paintings and statues are related to Gautama Buddha and his life. There are 153 Buddha statues, three statues of the kings of Sri Lanka and four statues of gods and goddesses. The second includes Vishnu and Ganesha. The murals cover an area of ​​2,100 square meters. The walls of the cave depict the temptation of the devil and the first sermon of the Buddha.

There are 2700 year old human skeletal burial sites in the Ibbankatuwa area near the Dambulla cave complexes. Prehistoric Sri Lankans may have lived in these cave complexes before Buddhism came to Sri Lanka.
